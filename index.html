A Celebration of tradition

Sumakah Festival - A Celebration of culture, food, and tradition

This is an annual activity that aims to promote the rich culture and tradition of the Antipoleños to the whole world as well as to establish camaraderie among the community and the city government. A Grand Parade, usually held on May 1, kicks-off the SuMaKaH (Suman, Mangga, Kasoy, Hamaka) Festival adorning the streets with street dancers and drum and lyre bands, all in their colorful costumes. After the parade, the crowd delights in a Street Dance Competition using the city’s official song, “Tayo Na Sa Antipolo!” Different shows and events are showcased in this month-long festivity prepared by the City Government.

Source https://www.rizalprovince.ph

Discover the Sumakah Festival

The Sumakah Festival is a vibrant cultural celebration held in Antipolo City. It highlights:

  • Suman – a beloved sticky rice delicacy
  • Mangga – sweet mangoes grown locally
  • Kasuy – Antipolo's famous cashew nuts
  • Hamaka – a symbol of community and tradition

It also celebrates Antipolo’s religious heritage and local industries.

Suman

Suman is a Filipino rice cake made from glutinous rice cooked in coconut milk, wrapped in banana leaves. It's a delicacy proudly made in Antipolo.

Mangga

Antipolo is known for its sweet, juicy mangoes, often enjoyed fresh or paired with suman and chocolate or sugar.

Kasoy

Kasoy (cashew) farming and processing is a significant local industry. Antipolo's kasuy is among the best in the country.

Hamaka

Hamaka refers to traditional wooden sleds once used in Antipolo. It also symbolizes the city's heritage and artistry.

The Significance of Sumakah Festival

The Sumakah Festival holds deep significance in the community of Antipolo, not only as a celebration of its rich cultural heritage but also as a symbol of unity. It brings together people from all walks of life, preserving traditional practices while embracing modernity. The festival:

  • Honors Heritage: Sumakah is a tribute to the long-standing traditions of Antipolo, showcasing the city's history and culture through food, art, and dance.
  • Promotes Local Industry: It shines a light on Antipolo's key industries, such as rice cake production (suman), mango farming, and cashew cultivation, supporting local businesses.
  • Religious Significance: The festival has roots in Antipolo's religious practices, particularly the veneration of Our Lady of Peace and Good Voyage (Nuestra Señora de la Paz y Buen Viaje), the city's patron saint.
  • Fosters Community Bonding: It serves as a platform for locals to reconnect, share stories, and celebrate what makes Antipolo unique as a thriving, vibrant city.

Overall, the Sumakah Festival is a celebration of community, tradition, and progress—a testament to the enduring spirit of Antipolo and its people.
