Hinulugang Taktak is a popular natural attraction in Antipolo, Rizal, featuring a scenic waterfall surrounded by lush greenery. Once a pilgrimage stop, it is now a protected landscape offering picnic areas, walking paths, and a refreshing escape for visitors.

Pintô Art Museum is a popular contemporary art museum located in Antipolo, Rizal. Founded in 2010, it features a wide collection of modern and indigenous Filipino art displayed across Mediterranean-style buildings surrounded by gardens. The museum promotes cultural awareness, environmental conservation, and artistic expression through its exhibits, open-air design, and serene atmosphere. It also includes spaces for performing arts, literature, and a café, making it a relaxing and enriching destination for visitors.

Antipolo Cathedral, officially the International Shrine of Our Lady of Peace and Good Voyage, is a historic church in Antipolo, Rizal. Built in 1632, it houses the 17th-century image of the Virgin Mary brought from Mexico. A major pilgrimage site, it was declared the first international shrine in the Philippines and Southeast Asia in 2024.

​Mount Purro Nature Reserve is a 36-hectare family-run eco-park located in Barangay Calawis, Antipolo, nestled in the foothills of the Sierra Madre Mountain Range. Established as part of a reforestation initiative, it now serves as a peaceful retreat promoting environmental conservation and community development. Visitors can enjoy activities such as guided hikes to scenic viewpoints like Malvar’s Peak, swimming in natural pools, and engaging with the indigenous Dumagat community through cultural exchanges. The reserve offers rustic accommodations, communal dining, and spaces for relaxation, making it an ideal destination for nature lovers seeking a tranquil escape from city life.​

​Cloud 9 Antipolo, officially known as Cloud 9 Sports and Leisure Club, is a scenic hilltop destination in Antipolo City, Rizal, offering panoramic views of Metro Manila, Laguna de Bay, and the Sierra Madre mountain range. Located along Sumulong Highway in Barangay Sta. Cruz, it has become a popular weekend getaway for both locals and visitors from Metro Manila
